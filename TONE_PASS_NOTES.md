# BLIP Tone Pass Notes

Target: stylized teen/young-adult sci-fi action adventure. BLIP keeps CONTACT-47, the Five Signal Scouts, colorful signal tech, humor, wonder and Chagrin Falls mystery, but the current slice should feel more dangerous, mysterious and forceful than cute or toy-like.

## High-Impact Issues Found

- Main menu and project copy leaned on "signal adventure" more than action/threat.
- Combat hits used light chirps and small sparkle-like bursts that made drones feel harmless.
- A few reward and field-note lines were explicitly cute or kids-club coded.
- AI-facing docs did not yet warn future agents away from bubbly UI, confetti effects or toy-like pickups.

## Changes Applied

- Main title subtitle now reads as a Chagrin Falls signal breach.
- README, Command Center and Claude game-director docs now describe BLIP as top-down sci-fi action adventure.
- Enemy hits and kills now add stronger sparks, mechanical rupture bursts, heavier shake and fading scorch marks.
- Procedural SFX shifted lower and harsher for carbine fire, enemy hits, explosions and pickups.
- Field notes and reward copy were tightened toward urgent Scout evidence and signal artifacts.
- Command Center art direction now tracks the mature-tone rule: keep color, add shadows, damage, smoke, emergency light, corrupted scars and forceful machine-damage effects.

## Deferred

- Full intro/cinematic rework.
- Dedicated damaged-building and abandoned-vehicle prop set.
- Optional stylized organic injury setting.
- New authored weapon/enemy assets.
